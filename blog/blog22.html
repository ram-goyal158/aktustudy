📵 How to Reduce Phone Addiction While Studying

Practical Tips for AKTU Students

🔍 Why Is Phone Addiction a Problem?

  • ❌ Reduces attention span
  • ❌ Kills productivity
  • ❌ Causes memory loss & stress
  • ❌ Leads to poor sleep and procrastination

✅ 1. Use the “Digital Detox” Rule

  • Turn off social media & OTT app notifications
  • Uninstall apps temporarily
  • Use Focus Mode (Android) / Screen Time (iOS)
📉 Saving just 2 hours a day = 60+ extra study hours per month

⏳ 2. Follow the 25–5 Rule (Pomodoro Technique)

Study for 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break (without phone!). After 4 sessions, take a 20-minute longer break.

📴 3. Make Your Phone Boring

  • Switch to grayscale mode
  • Move apps to the last screen
  • Use boring wallpapers like “Exams > Reels”
  • Enable Do Not Disturb during study blocks

📵 4. Study in a Phone-Free Zone

  • Keep phone in another room
  • Use a real alarm clock
  • Let a family member hold your phone
  • Study at a table, not in bed

🔐 5. Use App Blockers

🧠 6. Replace Phone Time with Productive Habits

  • 🎧 Listen to study music or motivational podcasts
  • 🧘 Do breathing or stretching during breaks
  • 📓 Write a journal entry or revise flashcards
  • 📖 Read 1–2 pages from a book

📅 7. Track Your Screen Time

  • Use Digital Wellbeing (Android) / Screen Time (iOS)
  • Set daily usage limit (1hr, 2hr max)
  • Reflect: “What did I miss today because of scrolling?”

👥 8. Join Focused Study Groups

  • Find serious study partners (Telegram/WhatsApp)
  • Set daily goals & share progress
  • Do silent Zoom/Google Meet sessions (Pomodoro style)

🔁 9. Set Social Media Boundaries

  • 🕗 No social media before 8 PM
  • Only 15 min/day for WhatsApp
  • Use One Sec App to delay impulsive taps

💬 10. Remind Yourself of the Bigger Goal

Stick this note on your phone:

“I can scroll later. Right now, I study.”

🎯 Every focused hour = better marks = better job = better life.
